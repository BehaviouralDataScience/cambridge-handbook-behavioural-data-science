About the Handbook

The Cambridge Handbook of Behavioural Data Science offers a comprehensive exploration of the emerging interdisciplinary field that bridges behavioural science and data science to understand, predict, and influence human, algorithmic, and systemic behaviour. It brings together scholars and practitioners from psychology, economics, computer science, statistics, mathematics, philosophy, engineering, and law, and is organised into modular parts — Introduction to Behavioural Data Science, Human Behaviour, Algorithmic Behaviour, Systems and Culture, and Applications — providing a non-linear map of the field's conceptual landscape and real-world significance.

Chapters examine behavioural decision-making, cognitive modelling, machine behaviour, algorithmic transparency, and the ethics of artificial intelligence, with applications spanning public policy, education, finance, health, cybersecurity, and sustainability — using techniques from agent-based modelling and Bayesian inference to natural language processing and quantum decision theory.

About the editors

Ganna Pogrebna is Professor at Queen's University Belfast and Senior Fellow at the Australian Strategic Policy Institute (ASPI). She is a pioneer of behavioural data science and behavioural AI, and the author of The Missing B in AI (2025).

Thomas T. Hills is Professor in the Department of Psychology and Director of the Behavioural and Data Science MSc at the University of Warwick, and the author of Behavioral Network Science (Cambridge University Press, 2024).
